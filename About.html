Key Club International History

Key Club International: Key Club International was originally formed in Sacramento, California on May 7, 1925. Key Club is a part of the six different branches within the Kiwanis Family, and is geared towards encouraging leadership in high schoolers and promoting service opportunities. The organization was founded by Albert C. Olney and Frank C. Vincent. In 1977, Key Club reached a breakthrough by providing female students the ability to serve under Key Club, allowing for the club to grow exponentially. Key Club International is made up of 5,000 clubs, 33 districts, 38 countries, and over 225,000 members! Key Club International’s mission statement is that “Key Club is an international, student-led organization providing its members with opportunities to perform service, build character, and develop leadership”.

CNH District History

CNH District: The California-Nevada-Hawaii District, also known as the CNH District is one of the 33 different districts within Key Club International. The District itself was founded in 1947 and follows the Motto of “Caring- Our Way of Life”. According to the name, it contains clubs all around California, Nevada, and Hawaii. With over 74 divisions, the CNH District is made up of different students and skills. The current goal for the CNH District is to raise $260,000, serve 900,000 hours, and recruit 38,000 members! The District Executives for the 2020-2021 term are: Daniel Min (District Governor), Grace Yim (District Secretary), and Yena Suh (District Treasurer). The headquarters for the CNH District are located in Rancho Cucamonga. The mascot for the CNH District is the Bees!

Region History

Region 3: Division 4 East is a part of the 7 different divisions within Region 3! Region 3 consists of Division 4 Central (Frogs), Division 4 East (Goldfish), Division 4 North (Otters), Division 4 South (Lions), Division 4 West (Dragons), Division 30 North (Rhinos) and Division 30 South (Suns). Each of these divisions has a respective color of the rainbow, those being red, orange, yellow, green, teal, blue, and purple. We are commonly known as Rainbow Region 3, and are located in Orange County. The Current Lieutenant Governors for the 2020-2021 term are as follows: Division 4 Central (Jacob Rice), Division 4 East (Vincent Vu), Division 4 North (Vivian Du), Division 4 South (Derek Ma), Division 4 West (Mai Vu), Division 30 North (Amy Wang), and Division 30 South (Jennifer Vo).

Division History

Division 4 East: Division 4 East is our Division! Our division mascot is the Goldfish, and our spirit color is orange. Our clubs are located around Anaheim, Orange, and Tustin. We currently have 10 different clubs within our division. The Lieutenant Governor of Division 4 East for the 2020-2021 term is Vincent Vu. Division 4 East was originally a part of Division 30 South some years ago, but eventually split apart in order to form multiple divisions within Region 3. Division 4 East has the current goals of raising $5,500 for the Pediatric Trauma Program (PTP) and serving 5,500 hours.
